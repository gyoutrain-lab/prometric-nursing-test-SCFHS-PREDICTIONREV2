SCFHS Prometric Test

21. A patient presents with sudden-onset chest pain radiating to the jaw. Which action is priority?

22. A post-op patient has a temperature of 39°C and purulent wound drainage. Most appropriate intervention?

23. A patient with COPD shows increased dyspnea and wheezing. Which action is immediate?

24. A diabetic patient has BG 50 mg/dL with confusion. Best immediate action?

25. A patient develops sudden shortness of breath post blood transfusion. First action?

21Q21 Rationale: Sudden chest pain suggests acute coronary syndrome; priority is ECG and code activation.
22Q22 Rationale: Fever and purulent drainage indicate infection; notify physician and obtain cultures immediately.
23Q23 Rationale: Increased dyspnea and wheezing in COPD needs bronchodilator promptly.
24Q24 Rationale: Hypoglycemia with neuro symptoms requires immediate fast-acting glucose if patient is conscious.
25Q25 Rationale: Acute transfusion reaction; stop transfusion immediately to prevent complications.
